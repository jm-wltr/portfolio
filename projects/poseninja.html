A webcam-powered action game where your body is the controller

What it is

A Fruit-Ninja-inspired game that uses real-time computer vision to turn your body into the controller. A webcam tracks your pose, renders your silhouette on screen, and you physically move to hit targets and dodge bombs. Supports single-player (three modes) and 1v1 split-screen. Released as a standalone macOS and Windows app on itch.io.

What I built

  • Real-time pose tracking — MediaPipe Holistic for body landmarks at 30fps with position smoothing to reduce jitter.
  • Body segmentation — YOLOv8 running in a background thread for pixel-level silhouette rendering and bomb collision detection.
  • Gesture-driven UI — full menu system controlled by fist open/close detection via MediaPipe Hands. No mouse needed.
  • Cross-platform packaging — bundled as native .app (macOS) and .exe (Windows) via PyInstaller, including YOLO weights and MediaPipe modules.
  • 2-player split-screen — independent pose trackers, per-player segmentation masks, and separate game state for simultaneous play from a single webcam.
